Accessing the Screen Recorder

To access the feature, follow these steps:

  1. Login to Video Cloud Studio and open the Media module.
  2. Click the Add video dropdown and select the Screen and Camera Recorder option.
  3. If this is your first time accessing the Screen Recorder, Terms of Use disclaimer will be displayed. Please read and accept the terms by checking the box and clicking Accept.
  4. Once the Terms of Use are accepted, the Screen Recorder is displayed in another tab.

Screen Recorder Overview

The Screen Recorder panel displays different options for the theme, background style, and recording configuration. Here, you will learn about each kind of action.

  1. Theme - Here you can choose between different themes or customize your own. Also, you can define the padding percentage.
  2. Background style - Customize the background by selecting different design styles.
  3. Recording - Configure your camera, microphone and choose what you want to record. Also, you can select the Canvas Size and Recording FPS.

Using the Screen Recorder

To learn how to use the Screen Recorder tool, follow these steps:

  1. Select 1 or more screens to record.
  2. In you use a microphone or camera, configure which you will use to record.
  3. Choose the Canvas Size and Record FPS.
  4. Configure the theme and background style that you prefer.
  5. Click on the red record button to begin the screen recording.
  6. Once the recording has finished, you can review and name the video, give a short description, and, if needed, add tags.
  7. Click Upload to save the video to your Media module.
When you stop recording, a video file of your recording will automatically be downloaded to your computer. The format and encoding of this file are chosen depending on your browser and its version.
Currently, video output depends on the browser you use to record. Downloaded videos may not work as expected due to how the browser records them.
Brightcove can guarantee it will upload and encode through Video Cloud, but Brightcove can't guarantee that it will play locally or through other video players or apps. If you need an offline file, you can download it from your Video Cloud account. See this topic for details.

What's the difference between microphone, tab, and system audio?

Microphone audio is the sound your computer's built-in microphone (or any external microphone you're using) captures during the recording.

Only record your voice with microphone audio. If you try to record the sound coming from your computer's speakers with your microphone, the quality will be very low.

Tab audio is any sound from within your browser that plays on your computer's speakers. For example, if you play a video on YouTube, the sound coming from the video is tab audio. Tab audio is only available when you record your browser tab.

We recommend using headphones if you want to record tab audio AND narrate over your recording. If you don't use headphones, your microphone may pick up the tab audio from your computer's speakers and distort the sound.

System audio is the same as tab audio, except it refers to all the audio originating from any of your computer's programs (not just a browser tab).

Performance recommendations

The following are a few performance recommendations to enhance the experience of using the Screen and Camera Recorder to ensure smoother performance and higher-quality outputs.

  • Do not minimize the newly opened window that holds the screen recorder while it's recording.
  • Do not add tabs to the newly opened window that holds the screen recorder.
  • Only run one screen recorder window at a time.
  • Screen and Camera Recorder is supported in Chrome, Firefox and Edge browsers.
  • Recording from phones and tablets is not supported.
