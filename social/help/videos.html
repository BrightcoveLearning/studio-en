Videos

Publish videos from your Video Cloud account to YouTube, Facebook and Twitter.

Before you can publish videos, you must create destinations for your social accounts. In the left navigation, click Destinations.

Video rendition

To distribute videos to a social platform, videos should have at least one of the following:

  • MP4 rendition
  • Video master

Videos uploaded using one of the standard ingest profiles will save a master by default.

When Brightcove Social publishes a video to a social platform:

  • Attempts to distribute the highest quality rendition that meets the rules of the social platform
  • If the rendition sent to the social platform is rejected, there is no attempt to send another rendition

Rules to rank videos:

  • MP4 assets are better than any other video container
  • The Video with the higher encoding rate is considered better
  • The video with the higher resolution is considered better
Publish to social media
  1. Select a video to publish.
  2. Click Publish.
  3. Select a social destination to publish to, or click Add New Destination.
  4. Review the video metadata properties for each destination that you are publishing to.
  5. Click Publish to Destination.

Manually publishing videos is a one-time operation, and these videos will not receive metadata updates from the Social module after the initial upload.

Set YouTube properties

By default, videos up to 15 minutes long can be uploaded to YouTube.

Video thumbnail source

  • YouTube - YouTube will select a thumbnail
  • Video Cloud - Use the Video Cloud thumbnail image as the YouTube thumbnail
  • Custom Image - Browse and select a custom thumbnail image to use

Checking Use for all destinations will use this thumbnail for all destinations currently being published to.

Updates to thumbnail images in Studio will not update the thumbnail image on the YouTube side.

Post Info properties

  • Title - Title of the video (defaults to Video Cloud title); YouTube supports up to 100 characters
  • Description - Description of the video (defaults to Video Cloud short description); YouTube supports up to 5000 characters
  • YouTube Category - Content category for the video
  • Tags - Tags for the video (defaults to Video Cloud tags); you can use max 30 characters per tag, and the text field accepts up to 500 characters

Schedule properties

  • Publish Immediately - To publish the video immediately
  • Schedule Publish Date - To choose a publish date and time for the video
The schedule date and time are sent to YouTube only once, when the video is sent to YouTube. If the schedule date and/or time needs to be changed, they must be changed in YouTube.

Captions properties

  • Use Video Cloud captions - Use captions associated with the video in Video Cloud
  • No captions - No captions will be sent to YouTube
  • Upload captions - Select a captions file, which should be in .VTT or .SRT format
Videos with closed captions clipped using Social will not have captions. You will need to upload new captions to the clipped video.

Visibility properties

  • Public - Anyone can view the video
  • Unlisted - Anyone who has the link to the video can view it
  • Private - Video can only be seen by you and the users you select

Selecting Notify subscribers when video is posted to YouTube may send a notification to your subscribers that the video was uploaded

License properties

  • Standard YouTube License
  • Creative Commons - Attribution

Set Facebook properties

Facebook does not allow .gif images. If your video is using a .gif for the thumbnail image, the publish to Facebook will fail.

Video thumbnail source

  • Facebook - Facebook will select a thumbnail
  • Video Cloud - Use the Video Cloud thumbnail image as the Facebook thumbnail
  • Custom Image - Browse and select a custom thumbnail image to use

Checking Use for all destinations will use this thumbnail for all destinations currently being published to.

Updates to thumbnail images in Studio will not update the thumbnail image on the Facebook side.

Post Info properties

  • Title - Title of the video (defaults to Video Cloud title); Facebook will truncate after 65 characters
  • Description - Description of the video (defaults to Video Cloud short description)
  • Tags - Select a Facebook tag from the list; Note that Facebook manages the tags
  • Facebook Category - Content category for the video

Schedule properties

  • Publish Immediately - To publish the video immediately
  • Schedule Publish Date - To choose a publish date and time for the video
The schedule date and time are sent to Facebook only once, when the video is sent to Facebook. If the schedule date and/or time needs to be changed, they must be changed in Facebook.

Captions properties

  • Use Video Cloud captions - Use captions associated with the video in Video Cloud
  • No captions - No captions will be sent to Facebook
  • Upload captions - Select a captions file, which should be in .VTT or .SRT format
Facebook requires that the caption language include a country in addition to the language. e.g., en-US or en-UK.
Videos with closed captions clipped using Social will not have captions. You will need to upload new captions to the clipped video.

Target properties

  • Audience Optimization Interests - Add tags to describe the interests of people who are most likely to enjoy your post
  • Location Restrictions - Add countries to include or exclude from seeing the post

Monetize properties

  • Who is your business partner for posts to this destination? - Add a business partner for branded content posts; As you type, a list of matching businesses will appear
  • Allow business partner to boost posts

Rights properties

Your Facebook account must have access to the Facebook Rights Manager for the Rights option to appear.
  • Detect Matching Content - Check to enable Facebook rights management
  • Specify Ownership - Specify copyright ownership on video, audio, or both
  • Country Selection - Select Worldwide, Include Countries or Exclude Countries
  • Apply Match Rule - Determines the automatic actions to happen when people upload matched videos; Match Rules are managed inside of Facebook
  • Grant Permission - Authorize certain people or Pages or Instagram accounts to have permission to post videos matching your content

Distribution properties

  • Show in News Feed - Shows video in News Feed
  • Allow Embedding - Allow video embeds on third-party sites
  • Add as Secret Video - Videos will be accessible only via a direct URL and are not searchable for people on Facebook
  • Turn off Reactions, Comments and Shares - Disallows likes and comments

Tracking properties

  • Leave Blank
  • Use Video ID from Video Cloud
  • Use Reference ID from Video Cloud, if present

Set Twitter properties

After a video is published to Twitter, any updates to the source video in Studio will not update on Twitter.

Post Info properties

Note that @mentions will be converted to #hashtags when being sent to Twitter.

Default video properties for Twitter can be set by editing the destination.

Create clip

Create video clips from existing videos in your Video Cloud account.

Clips can be used as short, teaser videos that can be posted to social media.

  1. Select a video to clip.
  2. Click Create Clip.
  3. Drag the controls to define the clip duration.
  4. Use the arrow keys to move frame by frame.
  5. Click Continue.

Download video

You can copy a video URL or download a video source file (highest quality MP4 rendition).

You cannot download videos that have DRM enabled.
  1. Select a video to download.
  2. Click Download.
  3. Copy the video URL or click the link to open the video in your browser.
  4. Right click the video to save the source file locally.
  5. Click Close.

It is important to understand that URLs for renditions and images are not fixed.

The URL is not intended to be a long-lived, publicly-accessible URL that you would paste into a public social platform post.

View in media

You can copy a video URL or download a video source file (highest quality MP4 rendition).

You cannot download videos that have DRM enabled.
  1. Select a video to view.
  2. Click View in Media.
  3. The Media module will open with the details of your selected video.
Check status

Videos published to social media will display Check Status in the Status column.

  1. Click Check Status for a video.
  2. You will see a list of social destinations that the video was published to along with the status.
Publish 360° videos

YouTube, Facebook, the Brightcove web Player, and the Brightcove SDKs support 360° video playback.

  1. Upload your video to Video Cloud. For details, see Working with 360° Videos.
    When using the Social module to sync a 360° video, the ingest profile used to ingest the video must be configured to save the master file.
  2. Return to the Social module.
  3. Select your video.
  4. Click Publish.
  5. Select a social destination to publish to, or click Add New Destination.
  6. Review the video metadata properties for each destination that you are publishing to.
  7. Click Publish to Destination.
Playback of 360° videos is limited to certain browsers.
Manually publishing videos is independent from Auto Sync. If a video is tagged to be published with Auto Sync, it will do so even if you have manually published the video.
